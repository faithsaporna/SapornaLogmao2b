Rules and Regulations

History

Susana Hot Springs, located in Malbog, Marinduque, Philippines, has a rich history and cultural significance. The hot springs have been utilized for their therapeutic properties and have become a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The history of Susana Hot Springs is intertwined with the cultural heritage of Marinduque. It is believed that the indigenous people of the island, the Mangyans, were the first to discover the hot springs and recognize their healing properties. Over time, the hot springs became known for their ability to alleviate various ailments, including rheumatism, arthritis, and skin conditions. The name "Susana" may have originated from a local legend or historical figure associated with the hot springs, although specific details about its etymology may vary depending on local folklore. In addition to their medicinal uses, Susana Hot Springs also hold cultural significance for the people of Marinduque. They are often visited for recreational purposes, such as swimming and relaxation, as well as for spiritual reasons. Many locals believe that the waters of the hot springs possess mystical or spiritual qualities, and they may perform rituals or ceremonies during their visits. Over the years, Susana Hot Springs have attracted visitors from across the Philippines and beyond, contributing to the tourism industry of Marinduque. Efforts have been made to preserve and promote the hot springs as a natural and cultural heritage site, ensuring that future generations can continue to enjoy their benefits and significance.
